Charminar


             Charminar                                                                       Local markets around Charminar

The Charminar is a beautiful monument and mosque in Hyderabad,India. It was sculpted in 1591 AD and is a center of attraction in Hyderabad. It is one of the most famous and renowned monuments in India. It was built to celebrate the end of a deadly plague of Mohammad Quli Qutb Shahi. The word Charminar is taken from the words 'Char' and 'Minar' which means 'four towers' in English.Charminar is not amongst the seven wonders of the world but it is an important and beautiful construction in the diverse history of India. The marvelous architecture and the essence of a member of the late history attracts a huge percentage of tourists every year and gives historic memories to them and leave them craving for more.The Local markets around Charminar are famous for bangles, silverware, attar or perfumes, sarees, and handwoven materials of silk, cotton, brocade, velvet and gold embroidered fabrics, among others.

In Telangana's heart, Rachakonda Fort is an intriguing tapestry of history, adventure, and natural beauty. This majestic fort tells tales of a bygone era, its walls whispering stories of courage and glory.The entrance of the Rachakonda fort serves as an outstanding example of monolith pillars. The uniqueness of this fort lies in its construction; it has been built without using any mortar in cyclopean masonry. The fort is also adored with beams and lintels with enthralling rock cut walls.Another striking feature of this temple is that it perfectly adheres to the principles of Vasthu Shastra, which influences most of the Hindu architectures. Kuntala Waterfalls

                                     Kuntala Waterfalls

Regarded as one of the most scenic waterfalls in Telangana, the Kuntala Waterfalls is situated in amid of gorgeous Sahyadri Mountain Range. At Kuntala,the Kadem river cascades 45 meters down and rushes deep into the Jungles. The road till the entry point of Kuntala Falls is passable and about 400 steps have to be trekked down to reach the bottom of the waterfall. It is known to be the highest waterfalls in the State that plunges from a height of 150 feet. The waterfalls offer spectacular sight particularly when the river is flush with copious inflows, thus making it the most popular weekend getaway from Hyderabad. Kuntala Waterfalls appear as a pleasant surprise, accessible through the twirling roads passing from dense forests that attracts visitors from far and wide. A temple dedicated to Someshwara Swami is located in the vicinity, where people come to pay their homage to the Lord.

Bansilalpet Stepwell

                                Stepwell-Bansilalpet

Built in the 17th Century during the Kakatiya Dynasty, the Bansilalpet stepwell was in use until the 1970's and went into a state of ruin due to negligence. Thanks to the efforts of the local government, the stepwell as cleared off of nearly 2200 tonnes of waste, restoring it to its former glory. In mid-2022, the revival and restoration of the stepwell began with the Telangana Urban Development Department as a partner. More than 2000 tons of debris was removed during the restoration process. The stepwell is a sight to behold in its restored glory. The light fixtures at several parts of the walkways, the beautiful water underneath and calming Carnatic music playing in the background made for the perfect setting for a peaceful evening here.
